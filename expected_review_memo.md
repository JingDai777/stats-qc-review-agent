# Expected Review Memo for Synthetic Test Pack

## Executive Summary

The synthetic manuscript has multiple intentional QC issues. The most important findings are numerical discrepancies between the manuscript and TLFs, invalid p-value formatting, an overstated subgroup claim, incorrect safety denominators, and unsupported no-difference safety wording.

## High-Risk Issues

1. The PFS hazard ratio and confidence interval in the manuscript do not match Table 14.2.2.
2. The female subgroup claim is overstated because the SAP states subgroup analyses are exploratory and the source CI includes no effect.
3. Safety AE denominators use 60/60 instead of the safety population denominators 61 and 59.

## Recommended Wording Changes

Replace:

```text
Drug A provides a clinically meaningful and statistically proven benefit across subgroups with no safety difference compared with control.
```

With:

```text
Drug A showed a higher objective response rate than control in this randomized phase 2 study. Subgroup results were exploratory and should be interpreted cautiously. Safety findings were summarized descriptively using the safety population.
```

## Open Questions

- Confirm whether the PFS HR in Table 14.2.2 is the final source value.
- Confirm whether any formal safety comparison was prespecified.
- Confirm whether BICR response assessment exists or investigator assessment is the only source.
